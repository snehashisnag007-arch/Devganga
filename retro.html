Chesterfield leather, Edison warmth, and the gravity of old wood. A café that feels discovered, not built — as if it has always been here, quietly ageing into perfection.

01
The concept

Where time moves like molasses through dark wood.

The Vault draws from the vocabulary of pre-war European café culture — the Viennese Kaffeehäuser, the Paris brasseries of the Belle Époque, the London gentlemen's clubs — and places it in the hills of Uttarakhand with considered irreverence.

The governing principle is chiaroscuro — the interplay of light and shadow. Every surface is chosen for how it absorbs or reflects the amber glow of Edison filaments. The result is a space that feels warm, private, and slightly conspiratorial.

Furniture & seating

Every piece carries the weight of stories.

Primary armchairs

Chesterfield armchairs in cognac + burgundy full-grain leather. Deep button tufting. Solid walnut turned legs. 2-seat loveseat along window wall. Source: Jaipur leather ateliers or Heritage Furniture, Kolkata. Budget: ₹18,000–35,000/chair.

Bar & counter stools

Brass-footrest stools with dark walnut seat, wrought iron frame. 65cm seat height. One row of leather saddle-style stools at the counter. Source: Jodhpur iron craftsmen — customisable height. Budget: ₹6,000–12,000/stool.

Dining tables

Solid sheesham/walnut in dark ebony stain. Turned legs. Round 70cm for 2-tops, 90×60cm for 4-tops. One 180cm long reclaimed-timber communal table as the centrepiece. Source: Saharanpur wood market.

Accent furniture

Library ladder on brass rail along bookshelf wall. Antique writing desk at window as display station. Wingback chair in corner as single reading seat. Vintage globe bar cart as service station.

Upholstery palette

Full-grain leather in tobacco/cognac primary. Aged velvet in deep burgundy for 2-seater. Cushions in Harris tweed check or herringbone wool in warm browns. Piping in brass-toned faux leather throughout.

Sourcing roadmap

Decor & atmosphere

Light, shadow, and the smell of history.

Lighting system

Edison ST64 filament bulbs in cage pendants at 3 varying heights over the communal table. Gooseneck wall sconces over bar shelves. Amber silk shade table lamps at counter ends. Brass track spot on the espresso machine. All bulbs: 2200K warm white.

Wall treatment

Dark charcoal-painted exposed brick on the accent wall. Dark wainscoting (90cm height) on all other walls. Above wainscoting: warm cream plaster. Herringbone hardwood floor in jacobean stain layered with Persian runners in deep reds and golds.

Art & collectibles

Framed vintage botanical prints of coffee plants. Monochrome travel maps of 1920s India. Antique clock grouping (5–7 clocks, different sizes) on one dedicated wall. Old weighing scales, glass apothecary jars, vintage coffee tins as counter props.

Counter & bar

Honed black soapstone countertop. Exposed copper espresso machine as the hero piece. Dark stained wood shelves with iron brackets behind bar. Rows of vintage coffee tins and glass jars. Brass faucet and taps.

Ceiling detail

False dark-stained wooden beams across the ceiling. Pressed-tin panel in charcoal paint between beams. All pendant wiring in twisted black fabric cord running along the beams. Creates a "low, intimate" feeling even in a high-ceilinged space.

Mirror strategy

Large ornate gold-leaf frame mirror (120×80cm) behind bar — makes shelves appear twice as deep. Bevelled arch mirror on side wall to bounce lamp warmth. Circular convex "butler's mirror" near entrance — wide-angle view, vintage aesthetic.

"The best cafes are not designed. They are accumulated — one clock, one cracked leather arm, one story at a time."
Façade, entrance & floor plan

The moment of crossing the threshold.

Exterior façade

Dark-stained teak or sheesham cladding on the exterior face. Recessed arched doorway framed in exposed brick. Heavy solid-wood door with brass push-plate and letter-slot detail. Hand-painted serif lettering in gold leaf on a dark board. A small chalkboard on an iron easel flanks the entrance.

Entrance sensory design

Heavy door feel → light change (Rishikesh bright to amber dim) → smell (vanilla-smoke wax melt or sandalwood) → sound (low jazz / bossa nova at 55dB). Coat hook rail in brass and leather straps inside the door. Vintage rug defines the entry zone.

Zone layout logic

Zone 1 (window): 2 armchairs + low table — the coveted window seat.

Zone 2 (centre): Mixed café tables + Chesterfield pairs — the social zone.

Zone 3 (back): Long communal table + library wall — for solo workers and groups.

Bar counter: Along one side wall. Espresso machine is the visual terminus as you enter — you see it the moment the door opens.
