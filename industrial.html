Cola trays become tables. Tyres frame mirrors. Cassette tapes cast light. Vinyl records become lamp shades. This is the café built from what others threw away — and it is the most alive of all four.

04
The concept

The most honest materials are the ones that have already lived a life.

The Yard has the highest viral potential of all four concepts. Each element is a conversation piece — a cola tray repurposed as a table, a motorcycle tyre holding a mirror, a cassette tape cluster casting warm light. Customers photograph these objects before they order. That is free marketing, and it compounds.

The governing principle is honest material — nothing is pretending to be something it isn't. The iron is raw. The wood is scarred. The cola tray still reads Thums Up. The beauty is in the second life, not the disguise.

Signature pieces — how to make them

The four objects that make this café unforgettable.

Cola tray tables

Source vintage Thums Up / Limca / Coca-Cola trays from antique shops, Chor Bazaar (Mumbai), or Dilli Haat. Weld 4 black iron pipe legs (32mm dia) to the tray underside — height 72cm for dining, 45cm for coffee table. The tray lip acts as the natural table edge. Sand lightly, seal with matte varnish. Stack spare trays on the wall between tables as art. Cost per table: ₹800–2,500 (tray) + ₹1,200–2,000 (welding + legs).

Cassette lamp pendants

Buy old cassette tapes in bulk from flea markets. Use 5–7 per lamp. String them on a circular wire frame (30cm dia) using 8-gauge steel wire, suspending tapes at different heights and angles. Run a pendant cord through the centre with an Edison ST64 bulb. The plastic reels scatter light warmly. Group 3 lamps at different heights over one table for maximum effect. DIY cost: under ₹500/lamp. The labour is the art.

Tyre mirrors

Source old motorcycle tyres (100/80 or 120/80) from scrap dealers — cleaned thoroughly. Spray matte black or copper with heat-resistant paint. Cut a circular mirror to fit inside the wheel rim at your local mirror shop (5mm glass). Mount mirror with strong adhesive + metal clips. Hang on a steel pipe bracket welded to the wall. Vary 3–4 sizes: scooter tyre, motorbike, car tyre. Cost per mirror: ₹600–1,500 total. This is your mirror strategy.

Vinyl LP lamp shades

Heat vinyl records to 120°C briefly in an oven to make them pliable — drape over a bowl form to create wavy/curved shape. Once set, drill a centre hole for pendant wire. The record becomes an organic-shaped lampshade — matte black, raw, and completely unique. Group 3 above one table at varying heights. Alternatively: frame individual records in shadow boxes as wall art. Cost: near zero if records sourced from scrap dealers.

Furniture & seating

Mismatched by design. Scavenged with intention.

Main seating — deliberate mismatch

Cola tray tables with 3–4 different chair styles at the same table — old school chair, metal factory chair, reclaimed wooden chair, cinema seat. The mismatch IS the design. Source from scrap dealers, old schools, cinema hall demolitions. Budget: ₹500–2,000/chair.

Bar counter stools

Old tractor seat welded onto a pipe pedestal as the hero bar stool. Alternatively: industrial steel-frame bar stools with weathered leather seat. A row creates the classic industrial silhouette at the counter. Tractor seats sourced from agricultural areas outside Rishikesh.

Pipe shelving system

Black iron pipe (25mm) with industrial flanges mounted to raw brick or dark-painted wall. Shelves in 3cm thick reclaimed teak or pine planks. This becomes the full display system for coffee equipment, vintage tins, and merchandise. Runs the full bar wall.

Cable reel tables

Large wooden cable reels sourced from electricity board surplus or construction sites. Stand upright (reel on side) = 60–80cm dia table. Leave raw or stain dark. Ideal as communal stand-and-drink tables near the bar or as feature tables with stools.

Reclaimed workbench counter

Thick solid wood workbench top (6cm), notched and scarred from a previous workshop life. Steel riveted edge trim. Below: black iron pipe frame. On top: old weighing scales, enamelware stacked, a vintage radio prop. Matte black espresso machine — no chrome, no gloss.

Wall treatments

Raw exposed brick primary. One wall: mural in black, white, and rust — abstract industrial shapes or a large blueprint-style drawing of a coffee machine cross-section. Vintage tin advertising signs (Parle-G, Britannia, old Bollywood posters) scattered across brick. LP records framed in a grid pattern.

"The cola tray remembers Thums Up. The tyre remembers the road. The cassette remembers the song. Now they all remember the coffee."
Façade & floor plan

The exterior should look like nothing — until it looks like everything.

Exterior façade

Raw exposed brick exterior, unpainted. Black steel plate letters for the café name (welded or riveted). A repurposed factory or corrugated iron door with heavy exposed bolts. Vintage petrol pump or old weighing scale at the entrance as a prop. A number plate collection above the door arch. Matte black window frames throughout.

Lighting system

Bare Edison ST64 bulbs on black twisted cord strung in zigzag patterns across the ceiling. Industrial cage wall sconces above the pipe shelves. Vintage angle-poise workshop lamp over the bar. All wiring runs in exposed black conduit pipe — adds to the industrial layering and makes the electrical system itself part of the décor.

Zone layout logic

Front zone: Cola tray tables — the statement zone near the entrance, under the Edison zigzag.

Centre-left: Cable reel tables + mismatched seating — the relaxed zone.

Wall left: Tyre mirrors + LP record art wall — the Instagram wall.

Wall right: Pipe shelving — the display zone.

Bar (back): Reclaimed workbench counter. Espresso machine is the focal terminus. Cola tray display wall behind the bar — doubles as art and as the bar mirror strategy.
